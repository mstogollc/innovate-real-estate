What We Do

Tax Strategies Built for Real Estate & Business Owners

Designed to minimize tax liability and maximize long-term wealth.

Retroactive Tax Deductions

Tangible Property Regulations

Optimize your tax position by uncovering missed deductions in previously filed tax returns and ensuring depreciation schedules work for you under the IRS Tangible Property Regulations.
Accelerated and Bonus Depreciation and PAD

Cost Segregation

Unlock significant tax savings by identifying and reclassifying assets to capture the full allowable deductions under IRS rules.
Soft Costs

263A UNICAP Exemption

Uncover additional deductions prior to cost segregation by identifying soft costs for significant tax savings through the 263A UNICAP Exemption.
Sustainable Drywall Solutions

Green Zip Tape

Sustainable drywall solutions designed to increase deductions by 5–10% of construction costs on both new construction and adaptive reuse projects. This approach reclassifies wall components from a 27.5- or 39-year recovery period to a 5-year asset class, accelerating depreciation and enhancing near-term tax savings.
Construction & Service Industries

Method Change

Enable companies to manage tax liabilities through strategic income deferral and expense timing under IRS accounting method changes.
Research & Development

R&D Tax Credits

Capture powerful tax credits for innovation, process improvement, and technical development within your operations.
Water, Power & HVAC Solutions

Energy Optimization

Reduce energy, water costs, and turn sustainable practices into measurable financial results.
Long-Term Planning

Wealth Strategies

Integrate tax planning with long-term objectives, drawing on an array of financial strategies to build lasting legacy wealth for you and your family.
Building Upgrades

179D

HVAC, LED lighting, roofing, and window components can be strategically evaluated and allocated toward energy-efficient tax deductions, helping maximize savings through improved asset classification and energy optimization strategies.
